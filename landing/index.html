The Problem

Managing facilities shouldn't feel like herding cats

Most facilities teams are running on duct tape and willpower — cobbling together spreadsheets, shared drives, and endless email threads just to stay afloat.

Spreadsheet Chaos

Lease dates, vendor contacts, and renewal options are scattered across a dozen spreadsheets. One wrong formula and you miss a critical notice period — costing you the right to renew.

  • Version conflicts between team members
  • No alerts for approaching deadlines
  • Manual data entry prone to error

Missed Deadlines

Lease expirations, HVAC inspection due dates, insurance renewals — these dates slip through the cracks. A missed 180-day notice clause can lock you into unfavorable lease terms for years.

  • No proactive alerts or reminders
  • Compliance dates buried in PDF attachments
  • One person holds all the institutional knowledge

Vendor Black Hole

You submit a maintenance request and it disappears. Vendors don't update you. You don't know if the HVAC unit was actually fixed or if the contractor is even licensed. Nothing is tracked end-to-end.

  • No visibility into work order status
  • Vendor documents stored in email attachments
  • No history of what was done and when
